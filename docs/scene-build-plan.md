# Medieval Fantasy Poker — Scene Build Plan

A requirements + placement spec to make the in-engine scene match the
**Room Blueprint** and **3D Stylized Visual Guide** references. Everything here is
expressed in both the blueprint's metric scale and our engine's world units.

---

## 0. Honest scope — what I CAN and CANNOT do

**I CAN (in code, with our assets):**
- Place/scale/rotate every asset at exact coordinates; build the room shell
  (walls, balcony, columns, floor, rug, ceiling, beams) procedurally to the
  blueprint's dimensions.
- Set up the full lighting rig (chandelier key light, warm candle points, cool
  daylight fill from the balcony, ambient bounce) with the blueprint's intent.
- Position the first-person camera to the spec'd eye height/seat.
- Add post-processing (bloom, tone mapping, vignette, mild SSAO) via three.js
  addons to push toward the rendered look.
- Build **procedural placeholders** for any missing prop (candelabra, candle
  stand, hanging chain, potted plant, side desk, bottle shelf).

**I CANNOT (needs you or an external tool):**
1. **Match the reference image fidelity 1:1.** Those references are offline-
   rendered / AI concept art (painterly GI, subsurface, hand-painted texturing).
   We render in **real-time WebGL** — we can get a strong stylized result, but it
   will read as a polished game scene, not as that concept art. This is the single
   biggest expectation to calibrate.
2. **Generate new 3D models.** I can't sculpt/texture new meshes. Missing assets
   (see §8 GAPS) must be generated by you (Meshy image-to-3D) — I'll wire in
   procedural stand-ins until you do.
3. **Author hand-painted PBR textures** to match the Materials & Textures sheet.
   I apply our procedural/imported materials; I can't paint new texture art.
4. **Improve the character sculpts** or rig/animate them (animation is already a
   separately-planned feature; the hook exists in `Character3D.playGesture`).
5. **Bake global illumination / lightmaps.** Real-time approximation only.

## 1. Coordinate system & scale

- **Up = +Y. North (balcony/vista) = −Z. South (YOU/camera) = +Z. East = +X.**
- **Scale anchor:** blueprint table Ø 2.70 m ≈ our table Ø 5.3 u, and blueprint
  seat radius 2.35 m ≈ our seat radius 4.5 u → **1 m ≈ 1.9 world units.**
- Use this factor for everything below. Key conversions:

| Blueprint | Metric | World units (×1.9) | Current value | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Room diameter | 16.0 m | Ø ~30 (r ~15) | walls at ±7 (Ø14) | **Enlarge room ~2×** |
| Room height | 4.8 m | ~9.1 | 6.5 | **Raise walls/ceiling** |
| Table diameter | 2.70 m | ~5.1 | 5.3 (furn_08) | ✓ keep |
| Seat radius | 2.35 m | ~4.5 | 4.5 | ✓ keep |
| Rug diameter | 6.00 m | Ø ~11.4 (r ~5.7) | r 5.0 | Bump to r ~5.7 |
| Chandelier height | 3.20 m | ~6.1 (above floor) | 4.3 | **Raise to ~6.1** |
| Player eye above table | 0.90 m | ~1.7 (eye Y ~2.9) | EYE Y 2.7 | Bump to ~2.9 |
| Balcony opening width | 7.20 m | ~13.7 | ~6.8 | **Widen opening** |

> The dominant gap: **the room is too small/low.** The blueprint is a grand hall
> with a wide prop ring (seats at 4.5 u, walls at ~15 u). We must scale the shell
> up; gameplay dims (table, seats, cards, chips) already match and stay put.

## 6. Lighting plan (blueprint "Lighting Guide")

Three layers, matching the legend's color coding:

1. **Chandelier key (warm, top-down):** PointLight at center, Y ~6.1 u,
   intensity ~3, color ~#ffd9a0, range ~14 u. Soft shadows onto the felt. This is
   the dominant table light.
2. **Warm candle points (3–5 m radius each ≈ 6–9 u):** small PointLights
   (~#ffb060, intensity ~0.6, range ~8 u) at every candle source — chandelier,
   floor candle stands (×2), wall candelabra (×4), lantern, candle cluster,
   table candles. Add subtle flicker.
3. **Cool daylight fill (from balcony, −Z):** DirectionalLight or large
   area-ish light coming through the arch, cool (#9fc7e8), intensity ~0.8, plus a
   HemisphereLight (sky #bcd0e8 / ground #3a3026, ~0.4) for ambient bounce.
4. **Tone mapping** ACESFilmic, exposure ~1.2–1.3 (already set). Optional bloom on
   candle flames + emissive.

> Notes from blueprint: keep the **center table area clear/bright** for card
> readability; ensure each AI is lit enough to read from the YOU seat.

## 10. Post-processing / fidelity (optional pass)

To close some of the gap to the renders (real-time, approximate):
- **Bloom** on candle flames + emissive gold.
- **ACES tone mapping** (set) + slight **vignette** + warm color grade.
- **Soft contact shadows** under table/props (some exist as decals).
- **SSAO** (mild) for grounding — costs perf; optional.
- These help the "mood/lighting" rows but won't reach concept-art fidelity (see §0).
